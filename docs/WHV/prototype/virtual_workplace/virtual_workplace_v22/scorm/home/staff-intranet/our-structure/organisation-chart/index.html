Organisation chart

Every individual in an organisation plays an important role in its operations and work culture. Explore the organisation chart below to find out what each role brings to GCBH.

CEO

The Chief Executive Officer (CEO) is the most senior member of an organisation. They are responsible for overseeing the organisation’s overall strategies, operations, and resources. Elected by the board of directors, they are the main communication point between the board and the rest of the organisation and public. They also lead specific initiatives that relate to whole of business practices such as sustainability.

Executive Assistant
The Executive Assistant provides administrative support to the CEO and the board, alongside supporting the internal and external communications of the CEO. They currently hold the voluntary staff elected role of workplace Occupational Health and Safety Representative.
Business Services Manager
The Business Services Manager supervises a team responsible for the business hub and recruitment services. They make sure that these services are properly equipped, are up-to-date, operate within budget, and meet community needs. They are also responsible for GCBH internal and external communications.
Business Liaison
The Business Liaison builds relationships with the community, working with local businesses to keep up-to-date on grants, sponsorships, and employment and training information and opportunities.
Event Coordinator (First Aid Officer)
The Events Coordinator is responsible for organising events for GCBH and for promoting the organisation. This includes planning, running, and evaluating workshops, seminars, and meetings. They are also responsible for communications strategies with key stakeholders.
Receptionist
The Receptionist is the first point of contact on the phone and manages communications and security with visitors. They answer and direct visitor inquiries to the appropriate parties and maintain a safe environment by following security procedures like logging and distributing visitor badges.
Employment and Training Manager
The Employment and Training Manager is the lead for a team dedicated to helping people develop the skills they need to be work-ready and employable.
Employment Advisor
The Employment Advisor works with people to help them identify and overcome barriers they may face when seeking and maintaining employment.
Training Coordinator
The Training Coordinator consults with businesses to determine what skills are in demand. They help jobseekers map out skills development and training paths to achieve job and career goals.
Community Programs Manager
The Community Programs Manager supervises a team that ensures that the GCBH programs are aligned to the needs of the community and values of the organisation and that they are running on time and on budget. They also lead specific initiatives that relate to community engagement such as community inclusion.
Community Arts Workers
The Community Arts Worker leads the arts education program at GCBH. They are responsible for leading arts programs that use an arts therapy approach to engage people with different art techniques and styles. They encourage participants to attend and share their work at local exhibits, festivals, and markets. They ensure the program reflects the diversity of the community and promotes positive mental health and living.
Community Sports Worker (First Aid Officer)
The Community Sports Worker oversees GCBH’s sports and recreation program. They promote and encourage people from the community to take part to develop skills and improve their health through physical activity and connecting with others. They ensure the program reflects the diversity of the community and promotes positive mental health and living.
Volunteers
Volunteers are individuals who give their time and skills to the community arts and sports and recreation programs.
Finance & Administration Manager
The Finance and Administration Manager heads a team that is responsible for GCBH’s business plan, budgeting for the organisation’s operations and programs and services, staff payroll, and filing taxes.
Accountant
The Accountant looks after GCBH’s financial records and operations. This includes writing the finance reports, filing tax returns, and managing accounts.
Quality Analyst
The Quality Analyst is responsible for making sure GCBH’s programs and services meet contractual requirements with funding bodies and donors, community standards, and the organisation’s visions and values.
Administration Support Officer
The Administration Support officer gives administrative and communications support to the finance and administration team.
Human Resources Manager
The Human Resources Manager leads a team responsible for all internal employee considerations and concerns. This includes managing and communicating staff benefits, training, compensation, and providing advice on any employee issues. They act as the induction coordinator for new staff and also lead specific initiatives that relate to staff such as gender equity.
HR Support Officer & OHS Coordinator
The HR Support Officer & OHS Coordinator provides administrative and communications support to all employees for any questions, comments, or concerns they have regarding HR topics.
Volunteer Coordinator
The Volunteer Coordinator is responsible for recruiting, training, and managing volunteers.
