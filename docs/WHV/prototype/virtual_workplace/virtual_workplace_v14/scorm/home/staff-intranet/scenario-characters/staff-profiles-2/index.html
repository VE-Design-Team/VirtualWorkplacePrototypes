Staff profiles

The decisions and actions a person makes is influenced by a multitude of things, such as their current situation, where and how they grew up, and their spiritual or cultural beliefs. The following profiles reveal more personal information about some of the key staff members at GCBH.

Select the images to learn more about some of the staff or use the arrows to move through the images.
CEO
Helen Omondi
Helen is in her mid-fifties, is of Kenyan descent, and married with no children. She spends a lot of her free time volunteering at her church and supporting the African communities in her area. Helen is passionate about social justice and social equity, which she has channelled in her work, leading programs in the social services sector. She is a hard-working and compassionate leader, but finds it challenging to have difficult conversations.
Phillip Martin
Human Resources Manager
Phillip is 56-years-old and married with three children – two daughters and a son. His relationship is traditional, with his wife staying at home with the kids and doing the domestic chores. Phillip is vehemently opposed to violence against women and wants to protect the women in his life. He sees the solution as being tougher on men who use violence and teaching women to protect themselves. Phillip has been feeling tired and irritable, which is affecting his work. His general practitioner has referred him to a psychologist, but Phillip is reluctant to make the appointment.
Jinni Bathiudeen
Receptionist
Jinni is in her late twenties, is married, and has a four-year-old son. She is heavily pregnant and is planning on taking parental leave shortly. She is a Muslim who wears a hijab.
Katarina Jovanović
Finance Manager
Katarina is 65-years-old. She was born in modern-day Serbia and migrated to Australia with her parents following WWII. Before joining GCBH, she worked in a variety of corporate roles in male-dominated workplaces but took an extended break from work to look after her children. When her children were teenagers she separated from her husband and raised her children on a single income. She would like to retire soon but is worried about the amount of retirement savings that she has.
Trisha Gallo
Business Services Manager
Trisha is in her mid-forties and has two children in primary school. As a single mum, she often finds it hard balancing work and life, especially when work events and activities are scheduled late in the day or on weekends. She often relies on her family or pays for after school care for childcare support.
Vicky Fisher
Community Arts Worker
Vicky is in her early thirties, has low vision and can only see out of her left eye. Art is her passion that she loves to share at GCBH. She is also a talented sculptor and painter. Vicky is in a same sex de facto relationship. At home, she has been fighting with her partner who thinks she spends too much time at work and on her hobbies.
Brendan Taylor
Community Sports Worker
Brendan recently joined GCBH in a part time capacity. He is in his thirties and is a British migrant on a sponsored working visa. He has a university degree and is an accomplished swimmer and tennis player. Brendan is single and does not have any children. He identifies as a trans man; however, his passport still states he is a woman.
Glenn Early
Employment Advisor
Glenn is 34-years-old. He grew up in rural Australia but moved to the city in his twenties to go to university. He is married with two children. His wife is pregnant with their third child. Glenn is Aboriginal and leads the Reconciliation Action Plan working group at GCBH. He believes in equality but thinks it ‘goes both ways’ and assumes that violence is worse in ‘other’ cultures. He’s unsure about where gender fits among different types of inequality.
David Peres
Community Programs Manager
David spent many years as a social worker before entering GCBH as Community Programs Manager two years ago. David is in his forties and married with three children. His parents migrated to Australia from Chile when they were in their twenties. David grew up speaking Spanish at home but was picked on at school for his accent. He actively discourages his own children from learning and speaking Spanish because he wants them to fit in.
Paul Sorokin
Employment and training manager
Paul is a father of two teenage daughters and is currently going through a divorce with his wife of 17 years. Paul has worked in employment services for 20 years. He has had some staff management issues lately that have resulted in some aggressive outbursts. The CEO has asked him to undertake anger management training.
