1. GCBH gender equity strategy

As an organisation that is situated in a local government building and is supported by local government funding, we at GCBH must adhere to many local government requirements and expectations. As CEO, I am pleased to bring to you all the Municipal Association of Victoria (MAV) Gender Equity approach

After initial conversations with MAV, our management team has decided to develop a working group to investigate how we can integrate this approach into our practices.

What steps have we taken so far?

Our managers have received information from MAV and have been reading and speaking to staff about the Victorian Safe and Strong Gender Equity Strategy. As a team, we are working towards identifying where GCBH sits within this strategy and through that, what our role will be to enable gender equity in our workplace and how we engage with the community.

The management team at GCBH have had numerous conversations on the topic of gender equality in the workplace. They have consulted with their teams and some key stakeholders on what gender equity in the workplace means to them. What has become clear from these conversations is that everyone shares a clear understanding that gender inequality is a driver of violence against women and that removing gender inequalities is key. Where people differ in opinion and ideas are in the how – what should be done. We therefore need to do more work to inform our approach.

The management team has agreed to look further into what gender equality means within their programs, services and business units. This has involved some initial conversations with a state-wide women’s health service, Women’s Health Victoria, to help them better understand what to do. Through these discussions, the managers have identified important networks, such as the Regional Women in Business Network (RWBN), to partner with in this work. They will be pursuing further conversations with the RWBN to assist with the development of a gender equity strategy.

The management team has also attended training provided by the Gentown Chamber of Commerce and Industry that was facilitated by Women’s Health Victoria on Gender Equity in Action. Feedback from the managers highlighted the importance of gender equity training for all staff as it challenged their own gender values, biases and stereotypes.

What are we doing next?

At GCBH, we have committed to further gender equity discussions to inform our gender equity strategy. Our HR Manager, Phillip Martin, and the Finance & Administration Manager, Katarina Jovanović, will be drafting an initial gender equity strategy for discussion.

We will be appointing key staff members to a gender review team. The purpose of this team is to consult on the strategy and to turn it into policy and practice.

We will be directed by the following organisations, who have substantial information on this subject, for starting points and to guide our next steps: the MAV, the Workplace Gender Equality Agency and Our Watch Workplace Equality Respect Standards.

We will also be drawing on the Women’s Health Atlas for appropriate statistical data for the region to inform our approach.

At GCBH we are motivated to learn more about what constitutes good gender equity practice so that we can lead gender equity within the community. The board fully supports this work and the efforts of everyone involved. We know that our role in the community carries influence, especially with our partner organisations and for new emerging businesses accessing the business hub. We therefore want to take the time to get this right.

If any team member wants to get involved in this work, please let your line manager know.

Stay tuned for future updates.

Helen Omondi,
CEO
